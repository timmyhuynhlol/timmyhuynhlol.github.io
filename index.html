Varsity Basketball and Track and Field.

Timmy Huynh

For the past 3 years, being in track and field and basketball made me realize that leadership plays a key factor in life. Even though I was not given the title “leader”, I am still able to help my fellow teammates strive to become better and more successful. My goal as an athlete is to win and help improve the newcomers’ performance during games and help them become successful. During my sophomore year and junior year, I was able to go to finals and make it into The California Interscholastic Federation also known as “C.I.F”. Here I was able to demonstrate to my teammates that anything is possible and that you have to be willing to strive for success. I showed them what it was like to succeed and fail. I demonstrated that failure is the only way to succeed because during a race I fell, but managed to get back up and finish the race. When you talk about a leader you talk about people who are more experienced than you and better than you. With this being said, I would look up to the people who are more talented and experienced than me to gain more knowledge. For example some of the people who I would look up too would be: Usain Bolt, Kobe Bryant, and Michael Jordan because these people are the people that are willing to put time and effort into what they do best. I then use this skill and apply it the things I love to do and help other people to improve.
